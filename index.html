About

I'm Kit — I moved into product from the support-engineering trenches, and I kept the parts that made me good there: I actually understand the systems, and I'm relentless about the user's real problem.

For years as a Technical Support Engineer I lived where the product meets the person using it — which turns out to be the best seat in the house for learning what to build. I got tired of filing the same problems as tickets, so I started shipping the fixes myself: internal tools, documentation pipelines, RAG assistants. That builder habit never stopped. Nights and weekends it's games and creative tools; day to day it's whatever removes friction for a real user.

What I'm after now is the product seat, explicitly. Not because I'm done being technical — because being technical is my unfair advantage in it. I can talk to engineers in their language and to customers in theirs, and I'd rather prove that by building an artifact that reads correctly to both than by claiming it. That's this page.
