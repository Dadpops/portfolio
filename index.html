About

I'm Kit. I build things for a living and for fun, and I'm happiest when I'm making something that takes a real problem off someone's plate.

I came up through the support-engineering trenches, which turns out to be the best seat in the house for learning what's actually worth building. I got tired of filing the same problems as tickets, so I started shipping the fixes myself: internal tools, documentation pipelines, RAG assistants. That builder habit never switched off. These days it's products and tools during the day, and games and little creative worlds on nights and weekends. I run SolutionsKit, my own studio, as the home for all of it.

When I'm not at the keyboard I'm probably out on a mountain bike trail here in Colorado, or getting happily outnumbered at home by my wife and our three dogs: a shiba inu, a blue heeler, and an australian shepherd. I like building things that are useful, a little playful, and honest about what they do. That's the whole idea behind this page: the same work told two ways, so it reads right whether you think in outcomes or in architecture.
